About

A systems-minded student building toward finance and high-stakes decision work.

This website is the official online home of David Anderle. It is designed to collect projects, research directions, and written work in one place with a clear and consistent public identity.

I am a student at the University of Wisconsin–Madison with a background in informatics and a growing focus on quantitative finance, risk systems, and market behavior under uncertainty.

My interests sit at the boundary of finance, policy, and engineered systems. I am especially drawn to questions involving volatility, derivatives, information quality, institutional incentives, and the design of better decision frameworks in environments where mistakes are expensive.

Over time, I want this site to become a sharper public archive of how I think: not a personal blog in the casual sense, but a structured record of projects, ideas, and work that point in a coherent direction.

Focus areas

The themes I return to again and again.

These are not just topics of interest. They are the recurring questions shaping the projects I choose, the papers I read, and the kinds of systems I want to understand more deeply.

01

Quantitative finance

Volatility, pricing logic, leverage, derivatives exposure, and the structure of markets when incentives and information start to misalign.

02

Risk systems

How systems absorb stress, where hidden fragility accumulates, and how risk can be measured, allocated, and controlled before failure becomes visible.

03

Policy and market structure

Retail investor protection, platform incentives, transparency, and the design of guardrails that improve decision quality without removing agency.

04

Engineered decision systems

From software and data workflows to aerospace systems, I am drawn to environments where reliability, precision, and failure tolerance matter.

Retail Investor Protection in High-Volatility Trading

A policy-oriented research direction focused on transparency, app-level risk communication, and practical mechanisms for improving retail decision quality during speculative episodes. The underlying question is simple: how should platforms behave when volatility and behavioral pressure spike at the same time?

Quantitative finance Modeling

Volatility and Stress Mapping

An evolving project around mapping how different market conditions change the relationship between leverage, risk tolerance, and asset behavior. The aim is not prediction theater. It is a cleaner way to think about regime shifts and decision quality under pressure.
