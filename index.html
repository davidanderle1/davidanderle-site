Lead case study

Retail Investor Protection in the Post-Memestock Era

I developed a policy proposal on how brokerage interface design, volatility warnings, and social-media-driven investment content shape retail investor decisions under stress.

Problem

Retail investing platforms often compress complex risk into frictionless interfaces exactly when users are most vulnerable to hype and volatility.

Approach

I analyzed the issue through platform incentives, disclosure timing, investor behavior, and decision architecture instead of treating losses as simple user error.

Outcome

Selected for the Wisconsin Ideas Conference policy memo track. It stands as my clearest public example of structured work on finance under pressure.
