About

A profile built around systems, not slogans

I am interested in what happens when markets become unstable, information quality drops, and incentives stop aligning with good decisions. That interest pulls me toward quantitative finance, risk systems, institutional design, and engineering problems where errors are costly and reasoning has to stay clear.

Positioning

Why this profile is different

The overlap matters. My work is not only finance-facing and not only technical. It sits in the space where models, incentives, systems, and execution all affect the outcome.

Intellectual identity

What I keep returning to

Volatility, derivatives, market behavior, high-stakes decision design, and engineering-style thinking. I care about how systems behave when stress exposes what was weak all along.

Long-term direction

Where this is heading

Toward work that rewards rigor, independence, and analytical depth. That can mean finance, technical research, infrastructure, or ventures built around difficult systems problems.

Selected work

Projects, roles, and high-signal case studies

This section is intentionally selective. It prioritizes work that shows analytical direction, judgment, or execution quality. Where a project is still being developed, it is labeled accordingly rather than dressed up as finished research.

Policy Markets Selected for conference

Protecting retail investors in the post-memestock era

Policy memo focused on transparency in social-media-driven investment content, high-volatility risk warnings inside brokerage apps, and short-form investor education during periods of extreme speculative behavior.

  • Framed the problem through incentives, interface design, and information asymmetry rather than generic investor-blame narratives.
  • Built a clear decision-policy lens around what platforms can disclose, when warnings matter, and how users behave under pressure.
  • Selected for the Wisconsin Ideas Conference policy memo track.
Why it matters: it demonstrates structured thinking about markets where volatility, design, and human behavior collide.
Institutions Leadership

Associated Students of Madison Student Council

Student governance work that exposed how institutions allocate resources, reconcile competing priorities, and translate argument into actual decisions.

  • Strengthened structured argument, public-facing judgment, and the ability to separate signal from noise in group decision environments.
  • Made institutional design more concrete by showing how process quality affects outcomes even outside formal finance settings.
  • Useful because it adds a governance and incentives layer to otherwise technical interests.
Why it matters: serious analytical work is not only about models. It is also about how organizations decide.

Current build Quantitative finance

Volatility and risk research slate

Ongoing project direction around volatility forecasting, derivatives logic, backtesting discipline, and decision-support models for high-uncertainty environments.

  • Core questions include what forecast quality is actually useful, how to compare models honestly, and how risk should be communicated under stress.
  • Built as a research slate rather than presented as a finished result that does not yet exist.
  • Designed to become a stronger body of work over time through real data, documented methods, and clearer outputs.
Why it matters: it makes the long-term direction explicit without pretending the work is finished before it is.
