Short bio

50 words: David Anderle is a University of Wisconsin–Madison exchange student and a Czech University of Life Sciences Prague Informatics student focused on systems, risk, financial systems, and decision-making under uncertainty. His work centers on how fragile structures behave when pressure rises and how better models improve real decisions.

100 words: David Anderle is a UW–Madison exchange student and a Czech University of Life Sciences Prague Informatics student focused on systems under stress, financial-system fragility, market structure, and decision-making under uncertainty. His current public work combines policy-oriented analysis, institutional and finance-facing student roles, and a flagship systemic financial risk model designed to make contagion and hidden fragility easier to see. Across markets, organizations, and technical systems, his central interest is the same: what fails, what survives, and which models actually help when conditions worsen.

One-line version: David Anderle studies systems, risk, and financial fragility, with a focus on decision-making under uncertainty.
